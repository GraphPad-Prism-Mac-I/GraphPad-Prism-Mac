## 🔍 Overview

<a href="#graphPadPrism">GraphPad Prism</a> serves academic life scientists, pharmaceutical researchers, clinical researchers, and graduate students who need to analyze experimental data statistically and produce publication-quality figures — the core quantitative workflow of biomedical research.

The <a href="#graphPadPrism">academic research lab use case</a> is Prism's foundation — laboratory researchers who generate experimental data, need to analyze it with appropriate statistical tests, and need to produce figures for publication in peer-reviewed journals. Prism is the standard tool in many academic biology, pharmacology, physiology, and neuroscience departments because it matches the specific data types and analysis needs of wet-lab research. The <a href="#graphPadPrism">pharmaceutical and preclinical research</a> applies Prism to drug discovery and development data — dose-response analysis, pharmacokinetic curve fitting, efficacy comparison between treatment groups, and the statistical documentation that regulatory submissions require.

The <a href="#graphPadPrism">graduate student research workflow</a> benefits from Prism's statistical guidance — the built-in checklist for each analysis asking questions about the data that determine the appropriate test helps students without formal statistical training make correct statistical choices. The guidance doesn't replace statistical education but substantially reduces errors from inappropriate test selection. <a href="#graphPadPrism">Collaboration through Prism files</a> shares complete analysis files including data, analyses, and graphs in a single file — collaborators and reviewers receive the complete analysis context rather than graphs and tables extracted from the original analysis.</p>

<a href="#graphPadPrism">Graph type library</a> covers scatter plots, bar graphs, line graphs, survival curves, box-and-whisker plots, violin plots, heat maps, and many others — with the specific graph types that scientific publication uses rather than the business chart types that general graphing applications optimize for. <a href="#graphPadPrism">Color and formatting customization</a> fine-tunes every graph element — axis scales, tick marks, fonts, colors, line weights, symbol sizes, and legend formatting — for the precise visual control that journal figure requirements demand.

<a href="#graphPadPrism">Statistical results reporting</a> produces summary statistics tables, ANOVA tables, regression parameter tables, and other structured results output in formats compatible with Methods sections and supplementary materials. <a href="#graphPadPrism">Prism subscription and perpetual licensing</a> provides access through annual subscription or perpetual license options — flexible purchasing for individual researchers, academic institutions, and commercial organizations.</p>

The nonlinear curve fitting capability is technically the most sophisticated aspect of Prism and the feature that distinguishes it most clearly from general statistical packages for pharmacology and drug discovery. Fitting dose-response curves to the four-parameter logistic equation, enzyme kinetics to Michaelis-Menten, radioligand binding to hyperbolic equations — these domain-specific fitting models with the correct parameter interpretation and constraint options are built into Prism in a way that requires significant programming effort to replicate in general statistical software.

## 🙋 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What graph types does Prism include?</summary>

Scatter plots, bar graphs, line graphs, survival curves, box-and-whisker plots, violin plots, heat maps, and other scientific graph types appropriate for life science publication.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can multiple researchers collaborate on a Prism file?</summary>

Yes. Prism files contain complete analysis context — data, analyses, and graphs — for sharing with collaborators who can view and analyze the complete file.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Prism handle repeated measures data?</summary>

Yes. Repeated measures ANOVA and related analyses handle within-subject experimental designs.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What multiple comparison tests does Prism support?</summary>

Tukey, Dunnett, Bonferroni, Holm-Sidak, Sidak, and Fisher's LSD are among the multiple comparison corrections available.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is there academic pricing for Prism?</summary>

Yes. Check the GraphPad website for current academic and student pricing options.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Prism analyze survival data?</summary>

Yes. Kaplan-Meier survival analysis with log-rank testing is available for survival data analysis.

</details>
